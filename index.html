What are Atoms?

Everything is made up of atoms - indivisable, uncreatable (or destroyable) small particles. Atoms contain protons, neutrons and electrons. Protons and neutrons are located in the nucleus of the atom, while the electrons are found in the shells. Protons have positive charges, neutrons have neutral charges, and electrons have negative charges.


What is a Shell?

As explained earlier, electrons are located in the atom's shells. But what are they? Shells are invisible rings around an atom that contain electrons that orbit the nucleus. It is similar to how the planets orbit the sun. The sun would be the nucleus, the planets would be electrons, and the cycle of the planets' orbit would be the shell!


More About the Nucleus...

Rutherford called the positive particles in the nucleus protons. It was later discovered by Rutherford and James Chadwick, that there is another particle with no charge, also in the nucleus, called the neutron.
