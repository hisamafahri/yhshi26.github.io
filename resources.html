Resources

Research

  • arXiv: an open-access repository of electronic preprints and postprints approved for posting after moderation, but not peer review
  • Center for Open Science: a non-profit technology organization with a mission to "increase the openness, integrity, and reproducibility of scientific research."
  • Google Scholar: a freely accessible web search engine that indexes the full text or metadata of scholarly literature across an array of publishing formats and disciplines
  • NCBI: a branch of the National Institutes of Health
  • Research Gate: a social networking site for scientists and researchers to share papers, ask and answer questions, and find collaborators

Academic tools

  • Academic tree: a family tree of scientists and scholars according to mentoring relationships
  • Desmos: an advanced graphing calculator
  • Library Genesis: a file-sharing based shadow library website for scholarly journal articles, academic and general-interest books, images, comics, audiobooks, and magazines
  • Stack Exchange: a network of question-and-answer websites on topics in diverse fields
  • Wolfram Alpha: a computational knowledge engine
  • Wolfram Mathematica: a software system with built-in libraries for several areas of technical computing

Neuroscience

  • BNCI Horizon: a website that contains information about brain-computer interfaces
  • Neuromatch.io: an online community of computational neuroscientists
  • NeuroTechX: a non-profit organization whose mission is to facilitate the advancement of neurotechnology
  • OpenBCI: an open-source brain-computer interface platform
  • Society for Neuroscience: a professional society for basic scientists and physicians around the world whose research is focused on the study of the brain and nervous system

Programming

  • CodeKeep: lets you store and share bits of code and text with other users
  • GitHub: a provider of Internet hosting for software development and version control using Git
  • HackMD: an open source, collaborative Markdown editor
  • Overleaf: an online LaTeX editor that's easy to use
  • Obsidian: a knowledge base that works on local Markdown files

Interesting people

  • 3blue1brown: a math YouTube channel created by Grant Sanderson
  • Computerphile: sister channel to Numberphile, featuring videos about computer science
  • JPerm: speedcuber on YouTube
  • Krishna Shenoy: a neuroengineer with solid publications using brain-computer interfaces
  • Leios Labs: a math YouTube channel created by James Schloss
  • Minute Physics: an educational YouTube channel created by Henry Reich in 2011
  • Nile Red: a YouTuber who does cool chemistry
  • Numberphile: an educational YouTube channel featuring videos that explore topics from a variety of fields of mathematics
  • Taufik Valiante: a neurosurgeon and neuroscientist at the University of Toronto
  • Veritasium: a channel of science and engineering videos featuring experiments, expert interviews, cool demos, and discussions
  • VSauce: a YouTube brand created by internet celebrity Michael Stevens

Other nerdy things

  • Jekyll theme: an open source website static theme (currently in use)
  • Koen Deschacht's EEG Experiments: a record of experiments using OpenBCI
  • Sebi's Random Tech: a YouTube channel that looks at electronics (lots of ThinkPads)
  • Universal Paperclips: a 2017 incremental game created by Frank Lantz
  • XKCD: a webcomic created in 2005 by American author Randall Munroe
